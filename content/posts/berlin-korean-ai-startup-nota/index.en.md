### From KAIST Campus Venture to Discussing AI-Powered Intelligent Traffic Control Systems with Hamburg

COVID-19 hit just as Nota moved into Factory Berlin, the mecca of Berlin startups. The internal networking events we had looked forward to were cancelled or moved online, and the Factory courtyard, which used to be lit up all night, went dark. The ball pit, a favorite in Factory's relaxation area, was closed for hygiene reasons, and a 'barrier' called masks appeared even among people who used to greet each other and have tea. Most of all, as the number of daily visitors was limited, it began to lose its vitality.

In the midst of this, Factory conducted various experiments to revive networking, the unique function of the space. Factory Berlin's internal community channel, Slack, became even more active than before. Various communication rooms suitable for the situation, such as #-discuss-corona_rescue, were opened to exchange information on support for COVID-19 founders, and the community found ways to coexist on its own. The Factory management also launched various programs such as online one-on-one meetings by field, mentor-mentee connections, product experiences for startups using VR, and virtual exhibitions combining art and technology.

> **Nota's Entry into Berlin**

I built my network one by one by participating in as many online events as possible at Factory. I contacted people in my areas of interest individually via Slack messages to get to know them one by one. Then, I noticed a new company called 'newcomers Nota AI' at the entrance of Factory. Seeing that the headquarters address was Daejeon, Korea, I remembered hearing that a Korean startup named 'Nota' had recently entered Berlin. I was happy to see them nesting in Factory and reached out.

> **Nota, Possessing AI Model Lightweighting Technology**

Nota is a company that started as a KAIST campus venture in 2015 under the philosophy of 'making life more convenient and enriched with artificial intelligence.' After the match between AlphaGo and Lee Sedol, AI became a major trend. Among them, Nota is an AI deep-tech startup that provides **On-Device AI solutions** based on deep learning model lightweighting technology.

Simply put, existing AI-based services and products are mostly based on servers or the cloud because of the complex and large amount of computation inside the AI. Therefore, it is difficult to run on individual devices such as smartphones and small IoT devices, operating costs increase, and privacy issues can occur because data passes through the cloud. **Nota possesses original technology for AI model lightweighting, allowing it to run even in situations with low power and low specifications without performance degradation.**

In 2020, it succeeded in attracting Series A investment with the participation of Samsung Venture Investment, LG CNS, Stonebridge Ventures, and LB Investment, reaching a cumulative investment of about 10 billion KRW. It drew attention by attracting strategic investment from both Samsung Group and LG Group for the first time in Korea. Since then, it has established subsidiaries in the US as well as Berlin, expanding its business overseas. How and why did Nota come to Berlin?

> **Beyond Korea, To the World: Nota**

Myung-su Chae, CEO of Nota, judged from the beginning that Nota's technology would be competitive enough in the global market. In 2019, they participated in BMW Open Innovation and had already received favorable reviews from global companies. Therefore, CEO Chae's plan was to aggressively enter the global market and consistently build collaboration references with global companies simultaneously.

"We are a startup that started in Korea, but the moment we clarified our identity as a startup that does '**Edge AI**' rather than just a Korean startup, expanding overseas was a natural step. We especially focused on the European market. There is a lot of demand for personal information protection in Europe, and it is regulated and encouraged by laws and policies, so we judged that Nota's On-Device AI solution was worth a try."

CEO Chae shared his thoughts on the initial process of entering Berlin. Nota chose Berlin as its overseas base after participating in the accelerating and local entry support program of KIC Europe, an affiliate of the National Research Foundation of Korea under the Ministry of Science and ICT in Berlin. During the process of establishing a subsidiary, they considered both Amsterdam and Berlin, but eventually chose Berlin due to Germany's national power and the various AI-related supports available. Since then, they have been operating in 'Hubraum', a startup support space of Deutsche Telekom, Germany's largest telecommunications company.

Manager Hye-jin Lee, who is practically in charge of all operational tasks of the German subsidiary, actively suggested that Nota move into Factory Berlin. "First of all, it's the most important place in the Berlin startup scene, and I thought the fact that large companies like Siemens, McKinsey, and Porsche are in-house was one of the biggest advantages." Indeed, after moving in, she was impressed by the way Factory Berlin management arranged meetings between Nota and other companies and how matching managers actively supported networking when related industries or companies were mentioned.

> **Lightweighting Platform 'NetsPresso'**

Nota initially focused on vision-based AI in mobility, security monitoring, and retail, and then launched the **lightweighting platform 'NetsPresso'**. Through this, they operated an **Intelligent Traffic System (ITS)** pilot project with Pyeongtaek City, Gyeonggi Province. In Germany, they are discussing with various municipalities that have smart city plans to apply ITS, including Hamburg, the leader in this field. Thanks to these active efforts, Nota was listed on the German federal government's AI startup map less than a year after establishing its German subsidiary and is active in the German AI field as a member of the Digital Hub Mobility in Munich and the Digital Hub in Karlsruhe.

CEO Myung-su Chae said that as many offline channels were closed due to COVID-19, it has been somewhat difficult to demonstrate and discuss technologies as closely as before. Previously, he would go on business trips every two months to meet companies and demonstrate technology directly, but recently, as things are conducted only online, business development with relatively conservative German companies has become more challenging, making the role of intermediaries like Factory Berlin even more critical.

As a Korean startup, Nota's presence in Berlin as an 'AI tech startup' while making full use of various support policies is no longer unfamiliar. The sentiment that used to fill me with pride when seeing a 'Samsung' billboard or a 'Hyundai' car while traveling in Europe in the past has now reached a level where I can nod and say, "Of course, our technology is ahead!"

I look forward to Nota's journey as both a Factorian and a Berliner.
