> **Inspiration from Factory Berlin CRO Catherine Bischoff and Founder Hana met through Matchmaking**

I joined Factory Berlin in February 2020. I had the opportunity to visit in December 2019, where I met **Catherine Bischoff, Factory's CRO (Chief Relationship Officer)**, who welcomed our visiting team. Catherine, who used to run an incubation platform in the Canadian startup scene, came to Berlin through her connection with Nico Gramenz, the current CEO of Factory Berlin, and has been leading Factory for about three and a half years.

![Catherine Bischoff, CRO of Factory Berlin. I applied to Factory Berlin with her as my role model. Photo = Factory Berlin](image1.webp)
*Catherine Bischoff, CRO of Factory Berlin. I applied to Factory Berlin with her as my role model. Photo = Factory Berlin*

> **Meeting Catherine Bischoff**

At the time, I was quite excited just to meet a C-level executive from one of Europe's leading startup accelerating institutions. The meeting was brief but intense. She kindly explained the **comfortable environment of Factory where one can occasionally bring children or pets to work, and the support programs for female founders within Factory**, using her own situation as an example. I felt that Catherine's dynamism made Factory shine even brighter. Afterwards, I decided to apply to Factory with the mindset of "I want to work in the same space as Catherine. I want to grow into a similar woman."

Although I didn't have a specific business idea at the time, I was contemplating how to make the most of my identity as a Korean. So my first goal was simply to become a member of a community and meet as many people as possible. Just as my entry into Factory was influenced by 'one person', I also wanted to be 'one person' who influences and is influenced by many people in this community. However, COVID-19 began right as I joined.

> **Networking through Matchmaking**

It was a bit desperate. All the various events I had dreamed of were cancelled, and there was no chance to gather and network after events. Looking back, for someone like me who is quite shy during first meetings, the stress of open networking parties might have been significant, so in a way, it was a relief. Instead, Factory held two rounds of online matchmaking events, through which I was able to meet a variety of people.

![Factory Berlin provides various meetings such as 1-on-1 matching and group matchmaking. After COVID-19, these have been replaced by online matchmaking. Photo = Factory Berlin](image2.webp)
*Factory Berlin provides various meetings such as 1-on-1 matching and group matchmaking. After COVID-19, these have been replaced by online matchmaking. Photo = Factory Berlin*

In the first round, after conducting a survey about 'my interests, my business plan, and the areas of people I want to meet', I was matched 1-on-1 with a French female consultant who could mentor me. **To me, who wanted to do work connecting Korea and Germany**, she, who connects France and Germany, gave realistic advice and supported the possibilities of what I could do.

In the second round, instead of 1-on-1 matchmaking, group matchmaking was conducted with groups of four. In my group, I met Shauna, a Canadian software developer, Truls, a Norwegian co-founder of an exercise music player & podcast company called [shift](https://shiftfm.app), and Hana, a French founder of an event and catering management company.

> **Am I the Center of Attention??**

We introduced ourselves in a group Slack chat room and talked about what each of us was interested in, what we could provide, and what kind of meetings we wanted. At first, it was an awkward atmosphere like a blind date, but interestingly, three out of the four people invited to this room were very interested in Korea, so I unintentionally became the 'insider' of that group. Most developers and people in the IT industry had a very good impression of Korea, seeing it as a 'tech-advanced country', and for those in culture or event industries, K-pop like BTS and BLACKPINK, and Korean food like Kimchi and Bibimbap were already the trendiest things. Soon after, I exchanged private messages with Hana, whom I was most curious about, and met her offline.

> **Meeting Hana, Another Networking Pro**

Hana studied in Shanghai, Madrid, and Paris before coming to Berlin, where she worked as a project manager at Uber and Airbnb before founding her own company, [Travelogue](https://travelogue.co). It is a company that comprehensively plans and manages spaces, food, and programs needed for team events, seminars, and company workshops. She plans programs suitable for the length of time, such as hourly, daily, or weekly, and creates different programs depending on the space, such as utilizing workshop spaces within the company, special event spaces outside the company, or distant travel destinations away from the company. Travelogue has a total of 6 people including Hana, a social media/content manager, an event manager, a sales manager, a partnership manager, and a photographer. Since Travelogue doesn't prepare everything from space scouting to catering on its own, **various partnerships are one of the most important elements in Hana's company.**

Because of this, when Hana first came to Berlin and tried to start her business, she participated in at least two meetings a day, sometimes up to five, to meet many entrepreneurs and find good catering companies for partnerships. She said she participated in various events in Berlin held in the evenings from Monday to Sunday without rest. While preparing for her business, she counted that she met about 4,000 people in a year, and through this, she was able to form partnerships with about 80 catering-related companies. Hana runs a Facebook group called 'Berlin Experience Makers', creating a community for people who plan various events in Berlin. In fact, while talking with Hana over tea at Factory Berlin, most of the people passing by would greet her.

> **"Connection Focused on People and Experience"**

![Hana, whom I met through Factory Berlin's group matchmaking. She founded Travelogue and plans events, seminars, and workshops. Photo = Travelogue](image3.webp)
*Hana, whom I met through Factory Berlin's group matchmaking. She founded Travelogue and plans events, seminars, and workshops. Photo = Travelogue*

Since Travelogue is a business directly related to events and the food industry, it suffered a significant blow after the outbreak of COVID-19. However, she said that since her focus from the beginning was not simply on external things like 'event planning' or 'catering', but on **"what kind of experience to give people,"** she was contemplating how to utilize this in the COVID-19 situation. So, despite the limited circumstances, she said her job is still to meet people, especially people of various nationalities and fields, and listen to their stories. I shared stories about Korean people in Berlin, their interests, and the Korean restaurant craze in Berlin, and I gave a small 'pitch' to Hana about the keyword **'connection'** that I wanted to do.

Since then, while continuing to lead Travelogue, Hana opened a separate website suitable for the COVID-19 era. **In other words, while Travelogue focused on providing 'employee experiences' to working people**, she started [MINDT](https://mindt.io), a business that provides psychological counseling, advice on improving remote work environments, and advice on self-development routines for employees who feel isolated and lack communication due to remote work during the COVID-19 era, together with a group of experts. In other words, with the idea that an individual employee's growth and happiness are ultimately directly linked to the company's growth, she provides team coaching to companies and developed a program tailored for the COVID-19 era that can be conducted entirely remotely.

MINDT has secured customers such as the co-working space WeWork and the AI chatbot building IT company Rasa, and its future growth is also expected. Above all, Hana's image of continuously developing her business by focusing on her core themes of **'people' and 'experience'** gave me a big hint.
