> **Low cost of living, techno music attracts youth... well-timed startup-friendly policies show 'effect'**

When did Berlin, once a 'city of poor artists', become a 'startup mecca'?

Berlin was one of the largest and most developed cities in Europe around 1870. Especially in the 1900s, the headquarters of famous banks were mostly located in Berlin, making it no exaggeration to say that Berlin led European finance. However, after the end of World War II in 1945, as Germany was divided into East and West Germany, the West Berlin area was surrounded by East Germany and became an isolated island. Major companies all left Berlin and moved to West Germany, and until the wall fell in 1989, Berlin fell into a poor city struggling economically.

![Berlin, which had slow economic growth due to its division, has now become the city with the most startups in Germany and the youngest city in the world.](image1.webp)
*Photo = Berlin, which had slow economic growth due to its division, has now become the city with the most startups in Germany and the youngest city in the world.*

After Berlin became the capital of a unified Germany, efforts for industrial reconstruction began again. Industries in the fields of service, technology, and creation, which can grow rapidly even without a foundation, settled in Berlin. Large companies in various fields such as Siemens, Deutsche Bank, Lufthansa, and Allianz established second headquarters, and Berlin gradually became economically vibrant. Of course, it was not yet a match for Frankfurt, the financial hub, North Rhine-Westphalia led by research and development-oriented technology industries based on prestigious technical universities, or Bavaria with economic power comparable to an independent country, but **after unification, Berlin gradually became an important city as it emerged as the center of politics.**

> **Young people flocking for low cost of living and techno music**

Meanwhile, compared to other German cities, **artists began to flock because of the low cost of living.** While rock music in Hamburg and electro-pop in Düsseldorf dominated the city's sound, techno music was undoubtedly the best in Berlin. Even during the division, art existed in Berlin. The techno music trend in Berlin, which began in the 1960s, was led by young people who wanted to avoid military service in West Germany. Musicians who gathered in dark clubs to play experimental music and enjoy improvisation developed this into a resistant cultural movement centered in the Kreuzberg area of Berlin.

In the 1990s, unique Berlin-only techno music led Berlin's subculture centered around several clubs. Young people who entered on Friday nights and enjoyed staying up all night until Monday morning flocked to Berlin's clubs, and soon Berlin attracted millions of tourists from all over the world. **As 'Berlin = a good place to play' became recognized, the young population increased rapidly.** Currently, 55% of Berlin's population is under 45, and it is a young city with an average age of 42.7. At the same time, it is emerging as an international city where young people from all over the world gather. People from 190 countries live in Berlin today, and 21% of the city's total population is of foreign origin.

> **Berlin's Image Transformation**

Berlin thought it was meaningless to take leadership in areas such as automobiles, finance, and bio/health that other cities were focusing on, and **set the city's policy direction to attract and actively support startups by highlighting this young, international atmosphere.** This coincided well with the global startup boom. Factory Berlin, established in 2014 in cooperation with Google, played a major role in attracting startups to the city of Berlin. **Many founders with ideas even if they had no money gathered in Berlin, and Berlin Partner for Wirtschaft und Technologie GmbH, an economic promotion agency under the city of Berlin, also took the lead in actively attracting startups by opening business support programs focused on 'startups'.** Thus, Berlin became the city with the most startups in Germany.

Berlin has an average of 40,000 business registrations per year, of which 500 are startups. Now, the title **'City of Founders and Artists, International and Young City'** suits it better than 'Poor but Sexy City'. 80% of Berlin's GDP comes from industries such as creative/cultural industries, tourism, media/information and communication technology, and transportation systems. Later, in 2017, Berlin was selected as the Internet of Things (IoT) and Fintech hub in Germany, becoming the city with the most IoT and Fintech startups in Germany. In other words, 295 Fintech startups, corresponding to 1/3 of all Fintech companies in Germany, are located in Berlin.

> **Berlin grown into a city of international and young ideas**

Since Brexit, European headquarters of global financial institutions have moved to Frankfurt in large numbers, so Frankfurt still holds the title of a traditional financial city, but **most new ideas start in Berlin.** N26, a unicorn that has advanced not only to Germany but also to the United States and surprised the global financial market, also started in Berlin. Vivid, which conquered the Russian financial sector and entered Europe, Penta, which makes it easy to create corporate accounts and manage them in connection with tax and accounting programs, and Trade Republic, an app for easy stock investment, are also from Berlin.

**As startups become important, many large German companies are also looking at Berlin to find innovation.** It has even become a trend to establish large companies' own startup accelerating institutions in Berlin. Bayer's Grants4Apps is looking for startups in the bio/health field, and Deutsche Telekom operates a co-working space called Hubraum to interact with companies that will lead future growth. In addition, Axel Springer, Germany's largest media company, operates the Axel Springer Plug and Play accelerating program in cooperation with Silicon Valley.

Life as a foreigner in Germany is not easy. Especially for someone who has experienced fast 'services' such as administrative services and delivery services in Korea, there are more than one or two points where you wonder "how this country is still a developed country" when first settling down. Germany also evaluates itself as lagging behind in 'digitalization' and lacking innovation. **In that sense, especially for Korean founders, Germany is a land of possibility that needs improvement and innovation everywhere.**

But Berlin is different among many cities in Germany. It has a lot of disorder, amateurs, and is a paradise for fledglings just starting out. The low cost of living, where you don't have to worry much about making a living even if you fail, is firmly supporting many people's challenges. It is a paradise for challengers who aim for various directions in life. In this sense, Berlin is the future of Germany.
